How do we recommend songs?

The algorithm for the playlist generation involves first querying for the 100 most popular artists, randomly choosing 5, and allowing the user to sort the 5 artists based on their musical preferences. This sorted list is then passed as input into the algorithm. The selection algorithm takes the userâ€™s highest ranked artist and finds all the artists that are similar to the highest ranked artist, ordered by their familiarity scores. It then takes the top 10 familiar artists that are similar to this highest ranked artist and randomly selects a song associated with each of the 10 artists that have been chosen. This repeats for the second highest ranked (for which the top 8 familiar artists are chosen), third highest ranked (for which the top 6 familiar artists are chosen), fourth highest ranked (for which the top 4 familiar artists are chosen), and fifth highest ranked (for which the top 2 familiar artists are chosen) artists such that the output of the algorithm is a recommended playlist of 30 unique tracks from familiar artists that possess a high degree of similarity to the artists that the user ranked.
